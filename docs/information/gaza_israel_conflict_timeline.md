# The Gaza-Israel Conflict: A Comprehensive Timeline
*October 7, 2023 - May 26, 2025*

## Introduction

This timeline documents the Gaza-Israel conflict from October 7, 2023, to May 26, 2025, presenting events from a Palestinian-centered perspective. It focuses on the humanitarian impact of the conflict, civil society responses, and international legal and moral arguments. The conflict has resulted in unprecedented destruction in Gaza, with tens of thousands of Palestinian civilians killed, critical infrastructure decimated, and a humanitarian catastrophe that has affected the entire population of the Gaza Strip.

The timeline chronicles the evolution of the conflict from its initial outbreak following Hamas's attack on southern Israel, through Israel's military response, to the expansion of hostilities beyond Gaza into Lebanon. Throughout this period, Palestinian civilians have borne the brunt of the violence, with widespread displacement, destruction of homes and essential infrastructure, and severe shortages of food, water, medicine, and other basic necessities.

International legal bodies, humanitarian organizations, and civil society groups have consistently raised concerns about violations of international humanitarian law, with multiple rulings from the International Court of Justice addressing allegations of genocide and ordering provisional measures to protect the Palestinian population. Despite these interventions, the humanitarian situation has continued to deteriorate, with evidence of systematic targeting of civilian infrastructure and the use of starvation as a method of warfare.

This timeline serves as a record of the key events, civilian casualties, infrastructure destruction, and legal and diplomatic responses throughout this devastating conflict, providing a chronological account centered on the experiences and suffering of the Palestinian people.

## Key Categories of Events

### Initial Attack and Military Response

The conflict began with Hamas's surprise attack on southern Israel on October 7, 2023, resulting in approximately 1,139 Israeli deaths and 250 hostages taken. Israel's military response included declaring a state of war, mobilizing 300,000 reservists, conducting extensive airstrikes, and launching ground invasions into Gaza. By October 28, 2023, Israel had begun a ground invasion of the Gaza Strip, and by September 2024, the conflict had expanded beyond Gaza to include Lebanon, with the assassination of Hezbollah leader Hassan Nasrallah marking a significant escalation in the regional conflict.

### Blockade and Humanitarian Crisis

Israel imposed a total blockade on Gaza beginning October 9, 2023, cutting off electricity, water, food, and fuel supplies. Defense Minister Gallant described it as fighting "human animals," while humanitarian organizations warned of catastrophic consequences for civilians. The blockade intensified in March 2025, bringing humanitarian efforts to a near-standstill and creating conditions that Amnesty International described as a "genocidal act." By May 2025, the entire population of Gaza (2.1 million people) faced critical food insecurity, with 470,000 people experiencing catastrophic hunger.

### Civilian Casualties and Displacement

The civilian death toll rose dramatically throughout the conflict. By November 5, 2023, OCHA reported 9,770 Palestinians killed, including 4,008 children and 2,550 women. By January 21, 2024, the death toll had surpassed 25,000, and by October 7, 2024 (the first anniversary of the conflict), it had reached 41,615 Palestinians, including 16,756 children. A Lancet study in July 2024 estimated the total death toll could exceed 186,000 when accounting for indirect deaths from infrastructure destruction, disease, and shortages of essentials.

Mass evacuation orders forced over 1.1 million Palestinians to flee their homes in northern Gaza in October 2023. By June 2024, approximately 90% of Gaza's population was displaced, and by March 2025, over 436,000 people had been forced to flee again, confined to ever-shrinking spaces with limited access to basic services.

### Infrastructure Destruction

Critical infrastructure was severely damaged or destroyed throughout the conflict. Gaza's sole power plant ceased operations on October 11, 2023, after running out of fuel due to Israel's blockade. By December 16, 2023, 21 of Gaza's 36 hospitals were completely non-functional, and by April 2024, the World Bank and UN reported total damage to Gaza's infrastructure at approximately $18.5 billion, representing 97% of Gaza's combined GDP. Two-thirds of Gaza's structures were damaged or destroyed, including hospitals, schools, and water facilities.

### Legal and Diplomatic Responses

International legal bodies responded to the crisis throughout the conflict. South Africa filed a case against Israel at the International Court of Justice on December 29, 2023, alleging violations of the Genocide Convention. The ICJ issued multiple rulings, including interim measures in January 2024 ordering Israel to prevent acts of genocide in Gaza and improve the humanitarian situation, and in May 2024 ordering Israel to immediately halt its military offensive in Rafah and maintain open border crossings for humanitarian aid.

Diplomatic efforts to secure a ceasefire continued throughout the conflict, with a temporary ceasefire implemented from November 24 to December 1, 2023, allowing limited humanitarian aid into Gaza and facilitating hostage-prisoner exchanges. Hamas accepted an Egyptian-Qatari mediated ceasefire proposal in May 2024 that Israel rejected as insufficient, and by August 2024, ceasefire negotiations remained stalled despite international pressure.

## Humanitarian Impact

By May 26, 2025, the conflict had resulted in:

- Over 52,928 Palestinians killed and 119,846 injured according to the Gaza Ministry of Health
- Approximately 90% of Gaza's population displaced, many multiple times
- 71% of the Gaza Strip within Israeli-militarized zones or under displacement orders
- The entire population of 2.1 million people facing critical food insecurity
- 470,000 people (22% of the population) experiencing catastrophic hunger (IPC Phase 5)
- Only 17 of 36 hospitals partially functional, with 66% of critical infrastructure damaged or destroyed
- At least 430 aid workers killed, including 305 UN staff
- More than 1,400 healthcare workers killed
- 62% of school buildings used as shelters directly hit
- Severe shortages of food, water, medicine, and fuel creating conditions of imminent famine
- 92% of infants and pregnant women not meeting nutrient requirements
- Two-thirds of Gaza's structures damaged or destroyed
- Total infrastructure damage estimated at $18.5 billion (97% of Gaza's GDP)
- Water production reduced to 7% of pre-war levels

## Conclusion

The Gaza-Israel conflict from October 7, 2023, to May 26, 2025, has resulted in unprecedented humanitarian suffering for the Palestinian people. The systematic destruction of civilian infrastructure, targeting of medical facilities and personnel, and severe restrictions on humanitarian aid have created conditions that international legal experts and humanitarian organizations have described as potential genocide.

The Palestinian civilian population has borne the brunt of this conflict, with tens of thousands killed, the vast majority displaced from their homes, and the entire population facing critical food insecurity. The destruction of Gaza's infrastructure has been nearly total, with damage estimated at 97% of Gaza's GDP, effectively erasing decades of development and creating conditions that will require years, if not decades, to rebuild.

International legal bodies, including the International Court of Justice, have issued multiple rulings ordering Israel to prevent acts of genocide and improve humanitarian conditions, but implementation has remained limited. Diplomatic efforts to secure a lasting ceasefire have repeatedly failed, with Israel expanding the conflict beyond Gaza into Lebanon in September 2024.

As this timeline demonstrates, the conflict has been characterized by disproportionate civilian impact, systematic targeting of essential infrastructure, and the use of methods of warfare, including starvation, that violate international humanitarian law. The Palestinian people continue to face an existential threat, with the humanitarian situation deteriorating to catastrophic levels and international mechanisms failing to provide adequate protection or relief.

The events documented in this timeline serve as a stark reminder of the human cost of this conflict and the urgent need for accountability, justice, and a sustainable political solution that respects the rights and dignity of all people in the region.
